Why People Run

Iraqi refugees have been forced from their homes because of persecution, war, disease or famine. People have been fleeing Iraq ever since the Kurdish rebellions in the Iraq-Iran wars of the 1980s.


1980-1988 — The Kurdish Rebellions of the Iran-Iraq Wars

1990 — The Iraqi Invasion of Kuwait

1991 — The Gulf War

2003-2011 — The Iraq-US Wars

2011-2017 — The Iraq Insurgency, Civil War and Terrorism (ISIS/Islamic State Group)

People flee Iraq because of the constant crises it is going through, from the wars with Iran to the Gulf War and then to their very own Civil War. But people often come back from their new home countries if Iraq seems hospitable again, as the fall of Saddam Hussein in 2003 marked the return of 30,000 Iraqi refugees in two years.

The current situation in Iraq is relatively stable, although people are still fleeing because of the unstable political and military conflicts, as well as the threat of Islamic State.
